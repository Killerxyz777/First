About: United States of America

Why you study in United States of America?

The United States stands out as a prime destination for international students seeking a high-quality education, access to cutting-edge research opportunities, and a diverse campus community. Studying in the USA offers much more than just academic rigor - it provides a chance to immerse oneself in American culture, gain a global perspective, and develop lifelong connections with people from all backgrounds.

From exploring iconic American cities to participating in internships and research programs, studying in the USA presents unique opportunities for personal and professional growth. With its esteemed faculty, renowned academic programs, and esteemed degrees, the USA attracts students who seek to expand their horizons and achieve academic and personal excellence.

VISA Process

Student Visa

The student visa for the United States is known as the F-1 visa, and it is intended for individuals who wish to study full-time at an accredited educational institution in the United States. To obtain an F-1 visa, students must first be accepted into a qualifying educational program and demonstrate that they have sufficient financial resources to cover their tuition, living expenses, and other related costs while studying in the United States.

STEM Course Visa Benifit's

International students who pursue STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) courses in the United States may be eligible for an extended period of Optional Practical Training (OPT) after completing their studies. The OPT program allows international students to work in the United States for up to 36 months after graduation, providing valuable work experience and potentially leading to employment opportunities and permanent residency in the United States.

Non STEM Course

International students who pursue non-STEM (non-Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) courses in the United States may still be eligible for the standard 12 months of Optional Practical Training (OPT) after completing their studies. Although the OPT period is shorter for non-STEM fields, pursuing non-STEM courses can still provide valuable education and skills that may lead to career opportunities and personal growth.

H1B Visa

The H1B visa is a non-immigrant visa that allows U.S. employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations that require theoretical or technical expertise. To be eligible for an H1B visa, the foreign worker must have a job offer from a U.S. employer and possess the necessary education, skills, and experience to perform the duties of the specialty occupation.
