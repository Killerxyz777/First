About: United Kingdom

Why you study in United Kingdom?

The United Kingdom is an immensely popular study destination among international students owing to its world-class universities, highly reputed academic excellence, and a diverse student community from around the world. Many universities in the UK offer a wide range of courses and research opportunities that are highly valued in the global job market.Apart from academic pursuits, the UK is a hub of cultural richness and has a vibrant cosmopolitan atmosphere that attracts students who wish to explore a different way of life and immerse themselves in a new culture. With a strong economy and a thriving job market, the UK also offers valuable career opportunities for international students after they graduate. All these factors make the UK an extremely appealing destination for students who wish to pursue their academic and career goals while experiencing a multicultural and diverse environment.

Highest Paid Occupations in the UK

The highest paid occupations in the UK are primarily in the medical and financial sectors. According to data from the Office for National Statistics, the highest paid occupation in the UK in 2021 was medical practitioners, with an average annual salary of £81,546. Other highly paid occupations in the medical field include dentists, pharmacists, and nursing professionals. In the financial sector, investment bankers, fund managers, and financial directors are among the highest paid professions, with average annual salaries ranging from £60,000 to £90,000. Additionally, highly skilled technical jobs such as software engineering and data science are also in high demand and can offer competitive salaries. However, it's worth noting that salaries can vary significantly based on factors such as experience, location, and industry, so it's important to research specific roles and companies to get a more accurate understanding of earning potential.

VISA Process

Student Visa

The UK student visa process involves several steps. First, prospective students must apply to a licensed UK educational institution and receive a confirmation of acceptance for studies (CAS) letter. They must then submit an online application, pay the visa fee, and attend a biometric appointment to provide fingerprints and a photo. Additionally, applicants may be required to provide evidence of their English language proficiency and financial support. Once the application is processed, a decision will be made and the applicant will be notified. If approved, the student can then travel to the UK and begin their studies.

Post Study Visa

The UK post-study work visa, also known as the Graduate Route, allows eligible international students who have completed their studies at a UK higher education institution to stay and work in the UK for up to 2 years (3 years for PhD graduates) after their course completion. To be eligible, students must have completed a degree at a UK institution with a valid Tier 4 student visa and have left the UK before the visa expired. Once the visa is granted, graduates can work in any sector or at any level and do not need a sponsor. The visa can also be extended or switched to other work visas if certain requirements are met, such as securing a job offer from a UK employer.
